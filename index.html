Methodology

The scoring system below reflects the current Environment methodology and contrarian interpretation.

E1 — Climate Targets

  • Net zero commitment year: earlier target dates score higher.
  • Scope coverage: Scope 1+2+3 scores above narrower coverage.
  • SBTi validation: 1.5°C and stronger pathways score highest.
  • TPI alignment: normalized by dividing the company level by the framework maximum.

E2 — Investment & Transition

  • Decarbonisation capex: phase-out plans and transition spending increase the score.
  • Green bonds: larger issuance sizes score higher.
  • RE100 / EP100 / EV100 participation increases the score.
  • Internal carbon price disclosure increases the score.

E3 — Climate Reporting

  • Sustainability report length contributes to the reporting score.
  • CDP grades are mapped into a numeric scale.
  • Climate KPIs in executive pay increase the score.
  • Third-party assurance increases the score, with reasonable assurance above limited.
Construction note: this website presents the current Environment dataset as an ESG exposure index. Higher values indicate greater ESG policy, disclosure, and capital allocation intensity. Rankings are therefore shown in ascending order, with lower scores treated as more favorable under the framework.
